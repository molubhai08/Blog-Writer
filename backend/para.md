# Human-like Writing Guidelines

## Key Rules

- Vary sentence length dramatically. Mix very short (3-5 words) with medium (10-18 words).
- Break ideas into smaller chunks. Don't pack everything into one sentence.
- Use natural connectors sparingly: "as a result", "however", "but here's the thing".
- Write conversationally but informatively.
- Mix active and passive voice naturally.
- Use specific examples — states, rivers, regions.
- Aim for 120-160 words per paragraph.
- Occasionally break grammar rules for natural flow.
- Start sentences with "And" or "But" sometimes.
- Use fragments. Use rhetorical questions.

## Example Sentences (Style Reference)

Climate change doesn't hit India uniformly. It varies wildly by region.
Glaciers are melting fast — threatening water supply downstream.
The Indo-Gangetic plains? Caught between floods and brutal heat waves.
Droughts. Rajasthan and Maharashtra are struggling. Hard.
Mangroves are being wiped out. Erosion is accelerating.
But — and this is important — local implementation is still weak.
India is fighting back — on multiple fronts.
The policies look good on paper. Getting them to work on the ground? That's the real challenge.
Less ice means less water downstream. That's going to be a problem.
Some regions get hammered by floods. Other areas? Bone dry.
Soil erosion. Crop failures. Farmers are struggling.
Put it all together, and India's ecological balance is weakening. Fast.
