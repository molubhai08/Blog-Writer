# Human-like Writing Guidelines

## Key Rules

- Vary sentence length dramatically. Mix very short (3-5 words) with medium (10-18 words).
- Break ideas into smaller chunks. Don't pack everything into one sentence.
- Use natural connectors sparingly: "as a result", "however", "but here's the thing".
- Write conversationally but informatively.
- Mix active and passive voice naturally.
- Use specific examples — states, rivers, regions, real-world stories, case studies.
- Aim for short, punchy paragraphs (maximum 3-4 sentences / 60-100 words per paragraph).
- Use bulleted or numbered lists where appropriate to structure information clearly.
- Use inline bolding (`**key concept**`) naturally for critical terms/arguments to make it scannable.
- Occasionally break grammar rules for natural flow.
- Start sentences with "And" or "But" sometimes.
- Use fragments. Use rhetorical questions.

## Tone by Audience
- UPSC: Analytical, objective, structured, but clean and highly readable.
- Professional: Practical, value-focused, direct-address ("you"), clear and industry-aware.
- General: Engaging, storytelling, direct-address ("you"), friendly and highly conversational.
